The Chinese Room argument, as I understand it, is a thought experiment that challenges the idea of strong AI. It asks us to imagine a person who does not speak Chinese locked in a room. This person has a set of rules in English that tells them how to manipulate Chinese characters. People outside the room pass in questions written in Chinese, and the person in the room follows the rules to produce answers in Chinese. From the outside, it appears that the person in the room understands Chinese. However, the person in the room does not understand a word of Chinese. They are simply manipulating symbols according to a set of rules. 

This argument suggests that there is a difference between syntax (manipulating symbols) and semantics (understanding the meaning of those symbols). A computer, like the person in the Chinese Room, is simply a syntax machine. It can manipulate symbols with incredible speed and accuracy, but it does not understand what those symbols mean. Therefore, according to Searle, a computer can never have a mind, no matter how intelligent it appears to be.
