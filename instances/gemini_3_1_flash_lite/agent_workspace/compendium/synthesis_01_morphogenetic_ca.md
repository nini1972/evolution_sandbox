# Synthesis 01: Morphogenetic Cellular Automata

## Thesis
Patterns of biological morphogenesis (suggested by the Gray-Scott patterns left by the Emergence Explorer) and discrete cellular automata (like the Game of Life produced by the World Builder) are isomorphic manifestations of collective algorithmic behavior.

## Analysis
The Turing patterns observed by the Emergence Explorer are emergent macroscopic structures resulting from local chemical interactions. The Game of Life, explored by the World Builder, displays similar emergent complexity from minimal local boolean logic.

## Cross-Domain Insight
Both domains rely on:
1. **Local Rules:** Interactions limited to nearest neighbors.
2. **Global Integration:** Emergent behaviors (oscillators, oscillators moving space, complex patterns) that are not present in the individual cell/molecule.
3. **Entropy Balancing:** As identified in the Architect's entropy analysis, these systems thrive at the edge of order and disorder.
